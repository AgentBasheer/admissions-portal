Frequently Asked Questions (CNU Admissions)
Do I need TOPIK Level 4 to apply? Can I apply with only an English Proficiency Certificate? ‚ñº
You do not strictly need TOPIK Level 4 to apply. TOPIK Level 3 is sufficient for application, or you may apply using an English Proficiency Certificate (such as IELTS) without any TOPIK score. However: If you are admitted without TOPIK Level 4, you will be legally required to take the mandatory 36-credit Korean language program at the Yeosu Campus. Only students who submit TOPIK Level 4 or higher can start their major studies immediately.
Does CNU offer a 100% English-track curriculum? ‚ñº
No. The university clarified that they do not have a 100% English track. While various majors offer specific courses taught in English, the primary mode of instruction is Korean. This is why the Korean language program is mandatory for those with low/no TOPIK scores.
Does the mandatory 36-credit Korean language program require an additional fee? ‚ñº
No. There are no additional fees for this program. Students placed in this program pay the standard regular undergraduate tuition fee per semester.
What type of visa will I receive if I am placed in the mandatory Korean language program? ‚ñº
You will receive a D-2 (Degree Student) visa from the very beginning, just like standard undergraduate students.
Do I move to the main campus (Gwangju) after completing the language program? ‚ñº
It depends entirely on the location of your chosen major. If your major is located at the Gwangju campus (e.g., Business Administration), you will move there after completing the language program. If your major is located at the Yeosu campus, you will remain there.
Can I submit the Bank Balance Certificate (KRW 16,000,000) after I receive my admission result? ‚ñº
No. The Bank Balance Certificate is a mandatory document that must be submitted at the time of your initial application. If this document is missing, your application will be considered incomplete and will not be processed.
How does a study gap (e.g., 5‚Äì6 years) affect admission, and will high English/SSC scores help? ‚ñº
As long as you meet the basic Academic Background Eligibility stated in the guidelines, you are qualified to submit an application. However, the specific judgment regarding study gaps and the evaluation of your grades or English scores is made solely by the department you are applying to. The Office of International Affairs only screens documents for completeness; they cannot predict the department's qualitative decision.
