Frequently Asked Questions (JBNU Admissions)
What is the "Cash Incentive" mentioned? ‚ñº
JBNU provides a unique living support fund for international students. You receive approximately 650,000 KRW in cash during Semester 1, and another 650,000 KRW during Semester 2, totaling ~1.3 Million KRW. Important: This is paid out after you arrive in Korea and create a Korean bank account, so it's not included in the initial launch budget but helps with ongoing living expenses.
How does the scholarship system work for Freshmen? ‚ñº
Freshman scholarships are based on your language score. Type 3 (IELTS 7.5 or TOPIK 4) waives 50% of Tuition II (the expensive part) only. Type 5 waives 100% of Tuition I (the cheaper part). Your IELTS 7.5 qualifies you for Type 3, which is the best value for Engineering majors. Note: The Type 3 scholarship only applies to Tuition Fee 2, not the complete tuition.
Is living in the dormitory mandatory? ‚ñº
It is not strictly mandatory, but highly recommended for the first year. The "Chambit Hall" package includes 3 meals a day (Mon-Fri) in the cost. This reduces your daily food expenses to almost zero during the week, making it financially superior to living off-campus.
What form is the interview? ‚ñº
The interview accounts for 20% of your evaluation. It is typically conducted via Zoom or phone for overseas applicants. They will ask about your study plan, motivation, and financial stability.
Can I apply without Apostille documents? ‚ñº
No. JBNU strictly requires Apostille (or Consular Authentication) for your High School Diploma and Transcripts. These must be submitted by the deadline.
Does a study gap (approx. 5‚Äì6 years) or a lower HSSC score (60%) disqualify me from admission? ‚ñº
No. Your academic background (SSC 92% and HSSC 60%) meets the basic eligibility requirements. Regarding the study gap, it is not considered disadvantageous, especially since you have a valid reason (supporting your family) and professional experience. Note: While your IELTS 7.5 is excellent, it primarily benefits English-track applications.
Are majors like Computer Science, AI, Electrical, or Electronic Engineering offered as English-track programs? ‚ñº
No. All these majors (CS, AI, Electrical, Electronic Engineering) are strictly Korean-track. The only designated English-track units at JBNU are:
  • School of International Studies
  • School of International Engineering & Science
